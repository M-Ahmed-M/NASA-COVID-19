About US

Without any doubt, when the COVID‐19 pandemic spread out, all eyes have focused on the food supply chain resiliency. Notably, agricultural and transportation restrictions have made a fluctuation in the food supply chains. As the chains must be continued overwide, there will be some changes in its mechanism. Thus, the team has decided to make a website that consists of NASA’s and the other space agencies’ resources as well as some familiar Q&As from different resources like FAO and WHO. Of note, Increasing the people’s awareness of this field is the team’s objective.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the pandemic affect the water supply the environment?

The COVID-19 virus has not been detected in drinking-water supplies. The risk to water supplies is low people can continue to use and drink water from their tap as usual. Disinfecting wipes and other items should be disposed of in the trash, not the toilet. Our critical water infrastructure and its operators ensure the safe supply of water depend on treatment chemicals, laboratory supplies. Know more.

Are there significant regional impacts (e.g. if a resource or activity is limited in a region, interruptions or closures can have large regional impacts on farmers and consumers)?

Blockages to transport routes are particularly obstructive for fresh food supply chains and may also result in increased levels of food loss. Fresh fish and aquatic products are highly perishable and need to be sold, or stored in a relatively limited time. Transport restrictions and quarantine measures are likely to impede farmers’ and fishers’ access to markets, curbing their productive capacities and hindering them from selling their produce. Shortages of labor could disrupt production and processing of food, notably for labor-intensive industries. Know more.



What is the difference between the food journey before and after the pandemic of covid-19?

Covid-19 threats the food production in the world and detonate a ‘hunger pandemic’. Food use in large-scale establishments, such as hotels, restaurants, sports arenas/stadiums and universities suddenly declined, the demand for food at grocery stores increased. Know more.



How do the pandemic of covid-19 affect the food journey through countries?

Airlines have grounded thousands of planes and ports have closed -- stranding containers of food, medicine, and other products on tarmacs and holding area said the UN Conference on Trade and Development on March 25. Countries that strongly rely on food imports, food security is vulnerable to revenues lost as a result of slowing economic activity caused by COVID-19. Know more.



How does COVID-19 impact on planting,harvesting,processing,and distribution of crops and livestock products across the world?

Globally, there is enough food for everyone. However, border closures, quarantines, and market, supply chain and trade disruptions are restricting people’s access to enough and nutritious sources of food. The most affected will be the poorest and most vulnerable segments of the population crises.



Will the pandemic affect the price and the number of crops?

The international prices of some major food staples have declined. The only staple food that has seen rising prices is rice. At local level, in markets, especially in countries already affected by hunger and other crises, some food commodity prices are increasing mainly because of local logistical problems or import difficulties. Know more.






Our solution




How should the governments deal with the food supply crisis, especially the predictable dilemma of starvation, that may be caused because of the COVID 19 pandemic?

Undoubtedly, the food supply chain must be continued all over the world during this pandemic. Food banks should be mobilized by the governments to provide food for the vulnerable households. Also, they should take a huge concern to the smallholder farmers, in terms of their awareness of precautionary measures, their accessibility to the finance and providing high value food collection centers.
